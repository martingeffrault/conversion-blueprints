# Performance / Growth Marketing Agency Recipe

> **Variant Focus**: Results-driven marketing, growth, ROI
> **Examples**: WebFX, Single Grain, Disruptive Advertising, Directive, Ignite Visibility
> **Key Differentiator**: Metrics, case studies with ROI, conversion-focused
> **Revenue Model**: Monthly retainers, performance-based, project fees
> **Target Clients**: Companies seeking measurable marketing results

---

## How This Differs from Creative Agency

| Aspect | Creative Agency | Performance Agency |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Hero | Visual, work-focused | Value prop, results |
| Portfolio | Visual essays | Metrics-heavy case studies |
| Services | Minimal, poetic | Detailed, specific |
| Trust signals | Awards, press | Client results, ROI |
| Content | Design thinking | SEO, tactics, how-to |
| CTAs | "Start a project" | "Get a free proposal" |

### Individual Service Page Structure

Service pages are critical for both SEO and conversion:

```
1. Hero
   └── Service name, brief description, CTA, trust signal

2. What We Do
   └── Detailed service description

3. Key Benefits
   └── Why this service matters (business outcomes)

4. Our Approach
   └── Methodology, what makes us different

5. Results
   └── Mini case studies or stats specific to this service

6. Pricing (optional)
   └── Starting at, or "request quote"

7. FAQs
   └── Service-specific questions (SEO value)

8. Related Services
   └── Cross-sell other services

9. CTA
   └── Get a proposal for this service
```

### SEO for Service Pages

> "Service pages are often the highest-converting pages on agency websites. They capture intent-driven search traffic from potential clients actively looking for a specific service."
>
> *Pattern from: [Search Engine Journal - Agency SEO](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/agency-seo/)*

**Target keywords**:
- "[service] agency"
- "[service] company"
- "[service] services"
- "best [service] agency"
- "[service] agency [location]"

## Case Studies: Metrics-Heavy

### Structure (Results-First)

Unlike creative case studies, performance agency case studies lead with results:

```
1. Hero
   └── Client name, headline with result, hero stat

2. At a Glance
   └── Client, industry, services, timeline, key results (summary box)

3. The Challenge
   └── Business problem, goals, previous situation

4. Our Strategy
   └── Approach, tactics, channels

5. The Work
   └── What we implemented (with visuals)

6. The Results
   └── Detailed metrics, graphs, before/after

7. Client Testimonial
   └── Quote about working with agency

8. CTA
```

| Type | Impact | Where to Display |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Client results (specific metrics) | Highest | Case studies, homepage |
| Client testimonials with title | High | Throughout site |

## Blog/Resources (SEO Content)

### Content Strategy

Performance agencies use content for SEO lead generation:

**High-intent content** (bottom of funnel):
- "[Service] pricing guide"
- "How to choose a [service] agency"
- "[Industry] marketing guide"

**Educational content** (middle of funnel):
- "How to [achieve outcome]"
- "[Tactic] complete guide"
- "[Year] [topic] trends"

**Traffic content** (top of funnel):
- "[Number] [topic] statistics"
- "What is [concept]"
- "[Tool] vs [tool]"

### Content Hub Structure

```
/resources/
├── /blog/                    All blog posts
├── /guides/                  Comprehensive guides (gated or ungated)
├── /case-studies/            (May duplicate main case studies)
├── /tools/                   Free tools (calculators, audits)
└── /videos/                  Video content, webinars
```

## SEO Strategy

### Target Keywords by Page

| Page | Target Keywords |
|------|-----------------|
| Homepage | "[type] agency", "[type] company", brand |
| Service pages | "[service] agency", "[service] services", "[service] company" |
| Location pages | "[service] agency [city]", "[city] [service] company" |
| Industry pages | "[industry] marketing agency", "[industry] [service]" |
| Blog | Long-tail informational queries |

### Local SEO (If Applicable)

If targeting local clients:
- Google Business Profile
- Location pages for each office
- Local schema markup
- "Near me" keyword variations
