# Accessibility & Compliance Library

> **Purpose**: Build inclusive websites that work for everyone and meet legal requirements
> **Usage**: Reference during design, development, and QA phases
> **Principle**: Accessibility is a feature, not an afterthought

---

## Available Guides

### [WCAG Guidelines](./wcag-guidelines.md)
Web Content Accessibility Guidelines implementation. Covers WCAG 2.1 AA requirements, POUR principles (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust), and practical implementation patterns.

### [Legal Compliance](./legal-compliance.md)
Legal requirements for websites. Covers GDPR, CCPA, ADA compliance, cookie consent, privacy policies, terms of service, and accessibility statements.

### [Accessibility Testing](./accessibility-testing.md)
How to test for accessibility. Covers automated testing tools, manual testing procedures, screen reader testing, and accessibility audits.

## Quick Reference

### WCAG 2.1 AA Checklist (Essentials)

| Principle | Key Requirements |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Perceivable** | Alt text, color contrast 4.5:1, captions |
| **Operable** | Keyboard navigation, focus visible, no seizures |
| **Understandable** | Clear language, consistent navigation, error prevention |
| **Robust** | Valid HTML, ARIA support, works across browsers |

### The Business Case

> "The Click-Away Pound Survey found that 71% of disabled customers with access needs will click away from a website that they find difficult to use."
>
> *Source: [Click-Away Pound](https://www.clickawaypound.com/)*

### Legal Requirements by Region

| Region | Regulation | Scope |
|--------|------------|-------|
| USA | ADA, Section 508 | Public accommodations, federal |
| EU | EAA, EN 301 549 | Public sector, large private |
| UK | Equality Act 2010 | All service providers |
| Canada | AODA, ACA | Public, 50+ employees |
