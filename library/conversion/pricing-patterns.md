# Pricing Page Patterns

> **Purpose**: Evidence-based pricing page design for maximum conversion
> **Impact**: Pricing page is where intent converts to revenue
> **Principle**: Reduce cognitive load, make the decision easy

---

## Pricing Page Statistics

### Key Benchmarks

| Metric | Average | Top Performers |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| Pricing page CVR (to trial) | 2-5% | 8-12% |
| Free to paid conversion | 2-5% | 10-15% |
| Annual vs. monthly selection | 30% annual | 50%+ annual |
| Time on pricing page | 30-60 seconds | 2+ minutes |

### Impact of Optimizations

| Optimization | Impact | Source |
|--------------|--------|--------|
| "Most Popular" badge | +20% selection | CXL Institute |
| 3 tiers vs. 5+ | Reduced choice paralysis | Paradox of Choice |
| Annual discount visible | +35% annual selections | ProfitWell |
| Social proof on pricing | +17% CVR | Unbounce |
| FAQ section | -20% support tickets | Intercom |
| Money-back guarantee | +19% CVR | Baymard |

## Optimal Tier Structure

### Number of Tiers

| Tiers | Best For | Risk |
|-------|----------|------|
| 2 | Simple products, B2C | May seem limited |
| 3 | Optimal for most | Industry standard |
| 4 | Enterprise + self-serve | Can be complex |
| 5+ | Complex products | Analysis paralysis |

**Rule**: Start with 3 tiers. Add a 4th only if you need both "free" and "enterprise."

> "Studies suggest that removing currency symbols ($) from a menu reduces the psychological focus on price, making guests more likely to spend freely."
>

|-----------|--------------|

| Decoy items | High-priced item makes others seem reasonable |
| Nested pricing | Price at end of description, not aligned |

| Strategic placement | High-margin items in "golden triangle" |
