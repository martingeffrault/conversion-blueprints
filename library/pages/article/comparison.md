# Comparison/Roundup Article Blueprint

> **Purpose**: Help readers choose the best product in a category
> **Format**: "The X Best [Products] of [Year]" or "[Product A] vs [Product B]"
> **Primary Goal**: Drive affiliate clicks to recommended products
> **Revenue**: Affiliate commissions

---

## Core Principle

Comparison articles are decision-stage content. Readers are ready to buy ‚Äî they just need help choosing. Your job is to:
1. Quickly surface the best options
2. Provide enough detail for confident decisions
3. Make it easy to purchase through your links

> "Most people don't want to read the whole article‚Äîthey just want the key information in bite-size summary to know whether it's right for them."
>
> *Source: [Lasso](https://getlasso.co/product-review-template/)*

## Section 1: Title + Meta

### Title Formulas

**Best X Roundup**:
- "The [Number] Best [Products] of [Year]"
- "Best [Products] [Year]: Tested and Reviewed"
- "Best [Products] for [Audience/Use Case] in [Year]"

**VS Comparison**:
- "[Product A] vs [Product B]: Which Is Better?"
- "[Product A] vs [Product B] [Year]: Complete Comparison"

### Meta Info to Include
```

Written by: [Author Name], [Credentials]
Reviewed by: [Expert Name], [Credentials] (if applicable)
Products Tested: [Number]
```

## Section 2: Introduction

Keep it brief ‚Äî readers want to get to the picks.

**Include**:
- What products you're covering
- Who this article is for
- Brief credibility statement
- How many products tested

**Length**: 100-200 words max

### Example

> "After testing 47 wireless earbuds over 200+ hours, we've found the best options for every budget and use case. Whether you need earbuds for the gym, commute, or work calls, we'll help you find the right pair.
>
> Our testing covers sound quality, comfort, battery life, and real-world performance ‚Äî not just spec sheets."

## Section 4: Methodology

Build trust by explaining how you evaluated products.

> "Sleep Foundation's research methodology page breaks down how their lab team tests. That test-driven format makes the content feel closer to Consumer Reports than a typical affiliate roundup."
>
> *Source: [Tetra Marketing](https://www.tetramarketing.io/p/site-analysis-wirecutter)*

### What to Include

**How You Tested**:
- Testing duration
- Testing conditions
- Equipment used
- Number of products tested

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Criteria 1 (e.g., Sound Quality): What you measured
- Criteria 2 (e.g., Comfort): How you assessed
- Criteria 3 (e.g., Battery Life): Testing method

**Expert Input** (if applicable):
- Expert name and credentials
- What they contributed

### Example

> ## How We Tested
>
> We tested each pair of earbuds for at least 40 hours across different use cases: commuting, working out, taking calls, and casual listening.
>
> **Sound Quality**: Evaluated across multiple genres using lossless audio sources.
> **Comfort**: Worn for 4+ hour sessions to assess fatigue.
> **Battery Life**: Measured real-world usage vs. manufacturer claims.
> **Mic Quality**: Tested in quiet and noisy environments.
