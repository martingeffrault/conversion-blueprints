# About Page Blueprint

> **Purpose**: Build trust, humanize your brand, tell your story
> **Audience**: Prospects evaluating you, potential hires, partners, press
> **Goal**: Convert skeptics into believers through authenticity and credibility

---

## Core Principle

The About page is not about youâ€”it's about why you matter to your visitor. Connect your story to their needs. Show them the humans behind the brand.

> "People don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it."
>
> *Source: Simon Sinek*

---

## Who Visits About Pages

| Visitor Type | What They Seek |
|--------------|----------------|
| **Prospects** | Trust signals, legitimacy |
| **Job seekers** | Culture, values, team |
| **Partners** | Credibility, business model |
| **Press/Media** | Story, founders, quotes |
| **Investors** | Vision, team, traction |

Design for all of them.

## Essential Elements

### Hero Section

**What to include:**
- Compelling headline (not "About Us")
- Subheadline expanding on mission
- Strong visual (team photo, office, product)

**Headlines that work:**

| âŒ Generic | âœ“ Compelling |
|------------|--------------|
| About Us | We're on a Mission to Change [X] |
| Our Company | Meet the Team Behind [Product] |
| Who We Are | Building the Future of [Industry] |
| About | From Frustration to Solution |

### Origin Story

**Key elements:**
- The problem that sparked everything
- The "aha" moment
- Early challenges and wins
- Why it matters

**Structure:**
1. **The Problem**: What frustrated you
2. **The Insight**: What you realized
3. **The Solution**: What you built
4. **The Result**: Where you are now

### Mission/Vision/Values

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Mission** | What you do daily | "Help small businesses get paid faster" |
| **Vision** | Future you're creating | "A world where cash flow isn't a barrier" |
| **Values** | How you operate | "Transparency, Simplicity, Customer-first" |

**Values format:**
```
Value Name
Brief description of what this means in practice
and how it guides decisions.
```

### Team Section

**For each member:**
- Professional photo
- Name and title
- Brief bio (2-3 sentences)
- Fun fact (optional, humanizes)
- Social links (optional)

**Order by:**
- Founders/leadership first
- Then by seniority or alphabetically
- Consider featuring key customer-facing roles

### Stats/Numbers

Numbers build credibility. Include:

| Metric | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Customers/users | "10,000+ companies trust us" |
| Team size | "A team of 50+ experts" |
| Years in business | "Serving customers since 2015" |
| Locations | "Operating in 30+ countries" |
| Results delivered | "$100M+ saved for customers" |

## Trust Elements

### For B2B About Pages

- Client logos (especially recognizable names)
- Industry awards/recognition
- Certifications (SOC2, ISO, etc.)
- Press mentions
- Investor logos (if well-known)

### For B2C About Pages

- Customer count/community size
- Media features
- Social proof from press
- Years in business
- Sustainability/ethics certifications

## CTAs for About Pages

The About page should lead somewhere:

| Visitor Intent | CTA |
|----------------|-----|
| Prospect | "See Our Products" / "Book a Demo" |
| Job seeker | "View Open Positions" |
| Partner | "Partner With Us" |
| Press | "Press Kit" / "Media Inquiries" |

Consider a dual CTA:
```
Ready to get started?          Want to join us?
[See Products]                 [View Careers]
```

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone

- Authentic, not corporate
- Confident, not boastful
- Human, not robotic
- Specific, not vague

### First Person vs Third Person

| Style | Example | Best For |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **We/Us** | "We believe..." | Friendly, relatable |
| **Company name** | "[Company] was founded..." | Formal, enterprise |

**Recommendation**: Mix both. Use "we" for values/beliefs, company name for history.

### Length

- Hero: 1-2 sentences
- Story: 200-500 words
- Values: 50-100 words each
- Bios: 50-100 words each

## Common Mistakes

### âŒ "About Us" as Headline

Boring. Tell visitors what makes you different.

### âŒ Wall of Text

Break up with visuals, sections, and breathing room.

### âŒ Generic Stock Photos

Use real team photos. Authenticity matters.

### âŒ No Faces

People connect with people. Show your team.

### âŒ Vague Mission Statements

"We aim to deliver value" means nothing. Be specific.

### âŒ No CTA

Don't let visitors leave without a next step.

### âŒ Outdated Information

Old team photos, old stats = lost trust.

## SEO Considerations

- Include location for local businesses
- Use structured data (Organization schema)
- Optimize for "[Company name] reviews/about"
- Include keywords naturally in story
- Link to other key pages

## Mobile Optimization

- Team photos should be visible (not tiny)
- Timeline should stack vertically
- Stats should be scannable
- Long bios should collapse/expand
