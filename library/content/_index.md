# Content & Information Architecture Library

> **Purpose**: Plan and structure content for findability, usability, and conversion
> **Usage**: Reference during discovery, planning, and content creation phases
> **Principle**: Structure first, content second — good IA makes content work harder

---

## Available Guides

### [Information Architecture](./information-architecture.md)
Structure website content for optimal user experience. Covers site structures (hierarchical, hub-spoke), URL patterns, navigation planning, content hierarchy, sitemap templates, and internal linking.

### [Content Strategy](./content-strategy.md)
Plan, create, and manage content that drives results. Covers content auditing, funnel-stage content types, voice/tone guidelines, governance, calendars, measurement, and maintenance.

### [Email Funnels](./email-funnels.md)
Complete email sequence templates by business type. Welcome series, abandoned cart, onboarding, nurture sequences, win-back campaigns—with copy templates, timing, and benchmarks.

### [Objection Handling](./objection-handling/playbook.md)
Convert hesitation into confidence. Covers LAER/LAARC frameworks, the 5 objection categories, price objection responses, website objection handling strategies, FAQ design, and response templates.

## Quick Reference

### The 3-Click Rule

Users should find any piece of content in 3 clicks or less from the homepage.

### Content-First Design

> "Design in the absence of content is not design, it's decoration."
>
> *— Jeffrey Zeldman*

Plan content structure before visual design.

### Information Scent

Every link should clearly indicate where it leads. Users follow "scent" of information — if the trail goes cold, they leave.
