# Stats & Numbers Section

> **Purpose**: Build credibility through quantified achievements
> **Position**: After hero, within trust sections, near CTAs
> **Goal**: Provide social proof via impressive metrics

---

## Core Principle

Numbers are concrete. Claims are vague. "We're great" is forgettable. "10,000+ customers" is memorable and verifiable. Use stats to transform trust signals into proof.

> "Specific numbers are more credible than rounded numbers. '$1.2M saved' outperforms 'millions saved' in trust tests."
>
> *Source: Conversion optimization research*

---

## What to Measure

### Customer/User Metrics

| Metric | Example | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Total customers/users | "10,000+ customers" | B2B, SaaS |
| Active users | "50,000 daily users" | Apps, platforms |
| Countries/regions | "Used in 120+ countries" | Global products |
| Customer types | "500+ Fortune 500 companies" | Enterprise |

### Performance Metrics

| Metric | Example | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Uptime | "99.99% uptime" | SaaS, hosting |
| Speed/time saved | "Save 10 hours/week" | Productivity tools |
| Money saved/earned | "$5M+ saved for clients" | Consulting, tools |
| Conversion rate | "3.2x higher conversions" | Marketing, CRO |

### Experience Metrics

| Metric | Example | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Years in business | "Since 2010" | Established brands |
| Team size | "50+ experts" | Agencies |
| Projects completed | "1,200+ projects" | Service businesses |
| Certifications | "200+ certified consultants" | Professional services |

### Satisfaction Metrics

| Metric | Example | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| NPS score | "NPS of 72" | SaaS, services |
| Customer rating | "4.9/5 on G2" | B2B products |
| Retention rate | "95% customer retention" | Subscription products |
| Satisfaction rate | "98% customer satisfaction" | Services |

## Animation Best Practices

### Count-Up Animation

- Trigger when stats enter viewport
- Duration: 1.5-2.5 seconds
- Easing: ease-out for natural feel
- Format numbers as they count
- Don't re-trigger on scroll back

### Fade/Slide In

- Stats cards fade up sequentially
- Stagger delay: 100-200ms between
- Total animation: under 1 second

### What to Avoid

- Auto-playing before visible
- Very slow animations (>3s)
- Constant looping
- Animations that distract from reading

## Common Mistakes

### ‚ùå Too Many Stats

4 is ideal, 6 max. More creates decision fatigue.

### ‚ùå Unimpressive Numbers

Don't show "10 customers." Wait until numbers are impressive.

### ‚ùå Vague Metrics

"Thousands" is weaker than "10,247."

### ‚ùå No Context

"99%" means nothing without knowing what's being measured.

### ‚ùå Outdated Numbers

Update regularly. "Since 2018" data in 2025 seems stale.

### ‚ùå Made-Up Numbers

Fake stats destroy trust if discovered. Be honest.
